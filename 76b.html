Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) Plant responses
  • Plants respond to environmental stimuli such as light, gravity, and touch.
  • These responses help plants survive and grow efficiently.
2) Role of hormones
  • Plants use hormones instead of a nervous system.
  • Hormones usually act locally within the plant.
3) Auxins
  • Auxins are plant hormones that control growth.
  • They are produced in the tips of shoots and roots.
  • They diffuse backwards through the plant.
4) Effect of auxins
  • In shoots, auxins stimulate growth.
  • In roots, auxins inhibit growth.
5) Phototropism
  • Response to light.
  • Shoots are positively phototropic (grow towards light).
  • Roots are negatively phototropic (grow away from light).
6) Gravitropism (Geotropism)
  • Response to gravity.
  • Shoots are negatively gravitropic (grow upwards).
  • Roots are positively gravitropic (grow downwards).
7) Auxin distribution
  • Auxins accumulate on the shaded side and the lower side.
  • This uneven distribution causes bending.
8) Growth response
  • In shoots: shaded side grows faster → plant bends towards light.
  • In roots: lower side grows slower → root bends downwards.
