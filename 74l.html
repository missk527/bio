74. Urinary System and Kidney Structure

Key Notes (English)

Adapted from the speech: kidney structure, nephron function, filtration and reabsorption.

1) Functions of the Kidneys
  • The kidneys remove waste products such as urea from the blood.
  • They regulate the levels of ions in the blood.
  • They control the amount of water in the body to maintain balance.
2) Blood Flow Through the Kidneys
  • Blood enters the kidneys through the renal arteries.
  • It leaves through the renal veins.
  • Continuous blood flow allows filtration and regulation.
3) Urine Pathway
  • Urine is produced in the kidneys.
  • It travels down the ureters to the bladder.
  • It is stored in the bladder and released through the urethra.
4) Structure of the Kidney
  • A kidney can be viewed in cross-section.
  • It contains many tiny structures called nephrons.
  • Nephrons are the functional units of the kidney.
5) Structure of a Nephron
  • The nephron contains tubules and blood vessels.
  • The tubule carries fluid that will become urine.
  • Blood vessels supply and remove substances.
6) Filtration in the Nephron
  • Filtration occurs in the glomerulus.
  • Fluid is forced into Bowman's capsule.
  • Only small substances pass through.
7) Substances Filtered
  • Water, glucose, amino acids, urea, and ions are filtered.
  • Large molecules like proteins and cells are not filtered.
8) Filtration Percentage
  • About 20% of plasma is filtered into Bowman's capsule.
  • The rest continues in the blood vessels.
9) Selective Reabsorption
  • Useful substances are reabsorbed into the blood.
  • Glucose and amino acids are almost completely reabsorbed.
  • Only some water and ions are reabsorbed.
  • Urea is not reabsorbed.
10) Parts of the Nephron
  • The nephron includes Bowman's capsule, proximal tubule, loop of Henle, distal tubule, and collecting duct.
  • Fluid leaving the collecting duct is urine.
  • Urine eventually exits via ureter, bladder, and urethra.
