Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) Light entering the eye

Light first passes through the transparent cornea, which refracts (bends) light. The cornea has no blood vessels, so oxygen diffuses in from the air.

2) Iris, pupil and lens

The iris is the coloured part of the eye and controls pupil size. The pupil is simply a gap that allows light through. The lens refracts light and can change shape to focus light onto the retina.

3) Retina and photoreceptors

The retina contains rod cells (black and white vision, sensitive in low light) and cone cells (colour vision). In darkness, rod cells dominate, so colour vision is reduced.

4) Fovea and optic nerve

The fovea is packed with cone cells and gives the sharpest vision. The optic nerve carries electrical impulses from the retina to the brain.

5) Iris reflex

In bright light the pupil is constricted (circular muscles contract, radial relax). In dim light the pupil is dilated (radial muscles contract, circular relax). This protects the retina and improves vision.
