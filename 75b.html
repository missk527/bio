Key Notes (English)

Topic: Kidney Failure

1) What is Kidney Failure?
  • Kidney failure occurs when the kidneys can no longer filter waste from the blood effectively.
  • It leads to the buildup of toxins and excess fluids in the body.
2) Functions of Healthy Kidneys
  • Remove waste products from the blood.
  • Regulate water balance and electrolytes.
  • Maintain blood pressure.
  • Produce hormones such as erythropoietin.
3) Types of Kidney Failure
  • Acute kidney failure: sudden loss of function.
  • Chronic kidney disease (CKD): gradual and long-term damage.
4) Causes
  • Diabetes and high blood pressure.
  • Infections or injury to the kidneys.
  • Blockages such as kidney stones.
5) Symptoms
  • Fatigue and weakness.
  • Swelling in legs and ankles.
  • Reduced urine output.
  • Nausea and confusion.
6) Effects on the Body
  • Toxin buildup in blood.
  • Imbalance of salts and fluids.
  • Can affect heart, brain, and other organs.
7) Treatment
  • Dialysis to filter blood artificially.
  • Kidney transplant.
  • Medication and lifestyle changes.
8) Prevention
  • Maintain healthy blood pressure and blood sugar levels.
  • Stay hydrated and eat a balanced diet.
  • Avoid excessive use of harmful drugs.
