Biology - Blood and Its Components

  1. The components of blood are red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma.
  2. Red blood cells are the most numerous cells in blood.
  3. Plasma is the liquid part of blood.
  4. White blood cells help protect the body from disease.
  5. Platelets help in blood clotting.
  6. The main function of red blood cells is to carry oxygen from the lungs to body tissues.
  7. Hemoglobin is the substance in red blood cells that carries oxygen.
  8. Oxyhemoglobin is formed when oxygen combines with hemoglobin.
  9. Oxyhemoglobin is formed in the lungs.
  10. Oxyhemoglobin splits into hemoglobin and oxygen in body tissues.
  11. The absence of a nucleus allows more space for hemoglobin and oxygen.
  12. This increases the amount of oxygen red blood cells can carry.
  13. A red blood cell has a biconcave disc shape.
  14. This shape provides a large surface area for absorbing oxygen.
  15. The cell described is a red blood cell.
  16. The function of white blood cells is to defend the body against infection.
  17. Some white blood cells are capable of movement.
  18. They destroy microorganisms by engulfing or consuming them (phagocytosis).
  19. Antibodies bind to pathogens and help destroy them.
  20. Antitoxins neutralize toxins produced by pathogens.
  21. White blood cells make up less than 1% of blood.
  22. White blood cells act by phagocytosis, producing antibodies, and producing antitoxins.
  23. Platelets are small fragments of cells.
  24. Platelets do not have a nucleus.
  25. Platelets act when there is a cut or injury.
  26. This process is called clotting.
  27. Clotting stops bleeding and prevents microorganisms from entering the body.
  28. It works by sealing the wound and blocking entry.
  29. Plasma is pale straw-colored.
  30. Plasma makes blood watery so it can flow easily.
  31. Cells suspended in plasma include red blood cells and white blood cells (or platelets).
  32. Plasma transports nutrients such as glucose and amino acids.
  33. Plasma also carries waste products like carbon dioxide and urea.
  34. Plasma transports hormones, proteins, antibodies, and antitoxins.
  35. The average adult has about 5 liters of blood.
  36. Blood loss can occur due to knife wounds or surgical complications.
  37. Saline solution is salt water.
  38. Saline solution increases blood volume so the heart can keep pumping.
  39. Saline does not contain red blood cells or carry oxygen.
  40. A blood transfusion is better because it provides red blood cells to transport oxygen.
