Bacterial Disease: Salmonella & Gonorrhoea

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) Bacteria: not all are bad
  • When we hear "bacteria" we often think of disease, but most bacteria are harmless or even useful.
  • Most bacteria do not interact with humans at all.
  • Many that do are helpful, for example:
    Bacteria in our intestines Help us digest food
  • In this lesson we focus on the harmful bacteria that cause disease.
2) General features of bacteria and disease
  • Bacteria are single-celled organisms (unicellular).
  • They are roughly 100 times smaller than human cells.
  • They can reproduce by themselves, without needing host cells.
  • Inside our bodies they often reproduce rapidly because there is a good food supply.
  • Many disease-causing bacteria produce toxins that:
    • damage our cells and tissues,
    • make us feel ill.
3) Salmonella: food poisoning
  • Salmonella bacteria cause food poisoning.
  • We catch Salmonella from contaminated food, especially:
    Infected chicken Other contaminated meat or food
  • Often the chicken picked up the infection while it was alive.
  • In some countries (e.g. the UK) most chickens are vaccinated against Salmonella, so infection is relatively rare.
Symptoms of Salmonella food poisoning • Fever (high temperature) • Stomach cramps • Vomiting • Diarrhoea Reason: the bacteria infect the intestines, so you get "tummy problems".
  • Usually the illness clears by itself within about a week.
  • Most people just need to:
    • stay well hydrated,
    • manage symptoms (e.g. have a sick bowl nearby).
4) Gonorrhoea: a bacterial STD
  • Gonorrhoea is a bacterial disease and an example of a sexually transmitted disease (STD).
  • It is passed on by sexual contact, e.g. having unprotected sex.
Main symptoms of gonorrhoea • Pain when urinating • Thick yellow or green discharge from the vagina or penis
5) Prevention vs treatment
  • For any disease, think of prevention and treatment as two separate ideas.
Gonorrhoea – Prevention • Avoid unsafe sex. • Use barrier methods of contraception (e.g. condoms).
Gonorrhoea – Treatment • Used to be easily treated with the common antibiotic penicillin. • Now many strains are resistant to penicillin. • Doctors must use rarer and more expensive antibiotics.
  • A strain is just a different type or variety of the same bacterial species.
  • Antibiotic resistance means the bacteria are no longer killed effectively by that antibiotic.
