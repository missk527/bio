44B. Methods of Contraception

Key Notes (English)

Rewritten from the speech: methods of contraception and how they prevent pregnancy.

1) How Pregnancy Begins
  • FSH stimulates an egg to mature in one ovary.
  • Around the middle of the cycle, LH triggers ovulation.
  • The released egg travels along the fallopian tube.
  • If a sperm meets the egg, fertilization can happen.
  • The fertilized egg then moves to the uterus and implants in the uterine lining.
2) The Main Idea of Contraception
  • Contraception prevents pregnancy by interrupting the normal process that leads to fertilization or implantation.
  • Different methods stop ovulation, block sperm, or prevent sperm and egg from meeting.
3) Hormonal Contraception
  • Hormonal methods use estrogen, progesterone, or both.
  • Steady estrogen prevents FSH from stimulating egg development.
  • Progesterone thickens cervical mucus, making it harder for sperm to enter the uterus.
4) Oral Contraceptive Pills
  • The combined pill contains both estrogen and progesterone.
  • It is over 99% effective when taken properly.
  • Possible side effects include headaches and nausea.
  • The progesterone-only pill is similarly effective and often causes fewer side effects.
5) Other Hormonal Methods
  • The contraceptive patch releases estrogen and progesterone through the skin and is changed weekly.
  • The contraceptive injection contains progesterone and lasts about 2 to 3 months.
  • The implant is placed under the skin of the arm and can work for up to 3 years.
  • A plastic IUD placed in the uterus releases progesterone and can also last for several years.
6) Copper IUD
  • The copper IUD does not use hormones.
  • It works by reducing sperm survival in the uterus.
  • Like the plastic IUD, it can remain effective for more than 3 years.
7) Non-Hormonal Methods
  • Male and female condoms stop sperm from reaching the egg.
  • Condoms are the only contraceptive method mentioned that also helps protect against STDs.
  • A diaphragm covers the cervix, but it is less reliable and should be used with spermicide.
  • Spermicides kill sperm, but used alone they are not very reliable.
8) Permanent and Natural Methods
  • Female sterilization blocks the fallopian tubes so eggs cannot travel to the uterus.
  • Male sterilization blocks the sperm ducts so sperm cannot be released.
  • These methods are usually considered permanent.
  • Natural methods such as timing sex or withdrawal are unreliable and not recommended.
  • Abstinence is the only method that is 100% effective at preventing pregnancy.
