82B. Family Trees & Cystic Fibrosis

Key Notes (Synced with Video)

0:00 - 1:25 | Cystic Fibrosis Genetics
  • Cystic fibrosis is a recessive inherited disorder.
  • The disease allele is represented by lowercase f.
  • The healthy dominant allele is represented by capital F.
  • People with ff have cystic fibrosis.
  • People with FF are unaffected.
  • People with Ff are carriers but do not have the disease.
1:32 - 2:15 | Family Tree Symbols
  • Circles represent females.
  • Squares represent males.
  • Shaded symbols show affected individuals.
  • Half-shaded symbols show carriers.
  • Unshaded symbols show unaffected individuals.
2:15 - 3:22 | Understanding Family Trees
  • People with cystic fibrosis are homozygous recessive.
  • Carriers are heterozygous.
  • Unaffected individuals are homozygous dominant.
  • Each row represents a new generation.
  • Horizontal lines connect couples.
3:22 - 4:45 | Genotypes in the Example
  • Jenny is unaffected and has genotype FF.
  • Paul is a carrier with genotype Ff.
  • Anna and Lucy are carriers.
  • Ron and Harry are unaffected.
  • Sam has cystic fibrosis and genotype ff.
4:45 - 5:51 | Probability & Punnett Squares
  • Lucy and Jack are both carriers.
  • Both parents have genotype Ff.
  • A Punnett square predicts offspring outcomes.
  • There is a 25% chance of cystic fibrosis.
  • There is a 50% chance of being a carrier.
  • There is a 25% chance of being completely unaffected.
