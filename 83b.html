Gregor Mendel & Genetics

Key Notes

0:00 - 0:47 | Gregor Mendel

  • Gregor Mendel is known as the father of genetics.
  • He was an Austrian monk and scientist in the 1800s.
  • He studied inheritance using pea plants.

0:47 - 1:25 | Pea Plant Experiment

  • Mendel crossed green pod plants with yellow pod plants.
  • All first-generation offspring had yellow pods.
  • Yellow pod color was dominant.

1:25 - 2:18 | Dominant and Recessive Traits

  • Crossing yellow offspring produced some green plants.
  • Recessive traits only appear when inherited from both parents.
  • Mendel called these hereditary units.

2:18 - 3:25 | Genes and Chromosomes

  • Mendel repeated experiments with many traits.
  • Scientists later linked genes to chromosomes.
  • Genes are inherited in dominant and recessive forms.

3:25 - 3:54 | DNA Discoveries

  • The DNA double helix was discovered in the 1950s.
  • The human genome was sequenced in 2003.
  • Modern genetics is based on Mendel’s discoveries.
