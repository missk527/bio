43. Reproductive Hormones and the Menstrual Cycle

Key Notes (English)

Adapted from the provided speech. Focus: puberty, the female reproductive system, the four stages of the menstrual cycle, and how reproductive hormones interact.

1) Puberty and Secondary Sexual Characteristics
  • Puberty is the stage when adolescents begin to develop secondary sexual characteristics.
  • Examples include facial hair in males and breasts in females.
  • These changes are triggered by reproductive hormones.
2) Main Reproductive Hormones in Puberty
  • In males, testosterone is produced in the testes.
  • Testosterone also helps stimulate sperm production.
  • In females, oestrogen is produced in the ovaries.
3) Female Reproductive System Structures
  • The uterus is the central organ where a fertilized egg can implant and develop.
  • The uterus lining builds up and breaks down during the menstrual cycle.
  • The ovaries store eggs and are connected to the uterus by the fallopian tubes.
4) Journey of the Egg
  • During ovulation, an egg is released from one of the ovaries.
  • The egg travels along a fallopian tube towards the uterus.
  • If it is fertilized, it may implant in the uterus lining; if not, it breaks down and leaves the body.
5) Menstrual Cycle Overview
  • The average menstrual cycle lasts about 28 days, although healthy cycles can vary between people.
  • The cycle involves four main stages controlled by four interacting hormones.
  • The graph used for the menstrual cycle often shows the thickness of the uterus lining over time.
6) Stage 1 – Menstruation
  • Menstruation is the period of bleeding caused by the breakdown of the uterus lining.
  • It usually lasts about 4 days.
  • On the graph, this stage is shown by the lining getting thinner.
7) Stage 2 – Rebuilding the Uterus Lining
  • After menstruation, the uterus lining starts to build up again.
  • It becomes a thick, spongy layer rich in blood vessels.
  • This prepares the uterus for a possible fertilized egg.
  • This stage lasts roughly 10 days, up to day 14.
8) Stage 3 – Ovulation
  • Ovulation happens around day 14 of the cycle.
  • It lasts for one day.
  • An egg is released from one of the ovaries.
9) Stage 4 – Maintaining the Lining
  • From after ovulation to about day 28, the uterus lining is maintained.
  • If no fertilized egg reaches the uterus, the lining begins to break down and the cycle restarts.
  • If a fertilized egg implants, pregnancy begins and the menstrual cycle stops.
10) Roles and Interactions of the Four Hormones
  • Oestrogen is produced by the ovaries and stimulates the uterus lining to grow.
  • Progesterone is also produced by the ovaries and maintains the uterus lining.
  • FSH and LH are produced by the pituitary gland.
  • FSH stimulates an egg to mature in the ovary.
  • LH triggers ovulation by causing the release of the mature egg.
  • FSH stimulates the ovaries to produce oestrogen.
  • Rising oestrogen inhibits FSH by negative feedback.
  • High oestrogen levels stimulate an LH surge, which causes ovulation.
  • Progesterone inhibits both LH and FSH.
