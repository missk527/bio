Notes (Key points rewritten from the speech)

Tumors vs Cancer — not the same
  • Tumor: an abnormal mass of cells formed by uncontrolled cell growth and division.
  • Two types of tumors: benign and malignant.
  • Only malignant tumors are classified as cancer.
Benign tumors
  • Cells are contained in one area, usually within a membrane.
  • They do not invade other tissues and do not spread around the body.
  • Therefore, they are not usually dangerous and are not called cancer.
Malignant tumors (cancer)
  • Cells can invade nearby tissues and spread to other parts of the body.
  • They may form new secondary tumors (metastases) in distant sites.
  • Much more dangerous and potentially fatal, which is why they are cancer.
Risk factors linked with cancer
  • Smoking → linked to lung cancer, and also mouth, stomach, cervical cancers.
  • Obesity → linked to bowel, liver, kidney cancers.
  • Ultraviolet (UV) light from the sun and sunbeds → strongly linked to skin cancer (UV damages skin cells).
  • Alcohol → linked to an increased risk of liver cancer.
  • Inherited genes → some variants increase susceptibility; BRCA genes are linked to breast and ovarian cancer.
