Notes (Key points from the speech)

What is cardiovascular disease (CVD)?
  • CVD is a general term for diseases of the heart and blood vessels.
  • Examples: coronary heart disease (CHD), heart attacks, faulty heart valves, and heart failure.
  • This lesson focuses on treatments: stents, statins, replacement valves, and replacement hearts.
Coronary heart disease (CHD)
  • CHD occurs when fatty material builds up in the coronary arteries that supply the heart muscle.
  • This narrows the lumen → less blood flows → less oxygen reaches the heart muscle → strain and possible heart attack.
Treating CHD: Stents
  • A stent is an expandable tube placed inside an artery to hold it open and keep blood flowing.
  • Benefits: relatively quick surgery; effective for a long time.
  • Risks: any surgery has risks (e.g., heart attack, infection); later, a thrombosis (blood clot) can form near the stent.
Treating CHD: Statins
  • Statins are a medication that alter the balance of cholesterol in the blood.
  • Cholesterol types: LDL (“bad”) and HDL (“good”).
  • Problem pattern: many people have too much LDL (promotes fatty deposits) and too little HDL (helps remove deposits).
  • Statins decrease LDL and increase HDL → lower risk of CHD, strokes, and heart attacks.
  • Downsides: must be taken regularly for years; can cause side effects such as headaches and kidney failure.
Faulty heart valves
  • Valves may be damaged/weak with age, after heart attacks, or due to infections.
  • Valves normally ensure one-way flow.
  • Problems:
    • Stiff valve that won’t open fully → not enough blood passes through.
    • Leaky valve that won’t close properly → blood flows backward.
  • Treatment: replace with biological valves (from a human or, more often, a pig/cow) or mechanical valves.
  • Both options require surgery and carry an ongoing risk of blood clots.
Heart failure and heart replacement
  • Heart failure: the heart cannot pump blood properly.
  • Some medications can help, but the only proper treatment is a new heart.
  • Replacement options:
    • Biological heart (donor human heart) — preferred definitive treatment.
    • Artificial heart — generally a temporary fix until a donor heart is found.
  • We cannot use pig or cow hearts for full heart transplants → must wait for a human donor (which can take a long time).
  • Even after a successful transplant, the heart may be rejected by the immune system.
  • Advantage of artificial hearts: made of metals and plastic, so the immune system doesn’t try to destroy them.
  • Sometimes a heart–lung transplant is needed; finding both organs is hard and the surgery is very complicated.
