Notes (Key points from the speech)

Two types of reproduction
  • Organisms can reproduce by sexual reproduction or asexual reproduction.
  • Sexual reproduction involves the fusion of two gametes, such as sperm and egg cells.
  • Asexual reproduction uses mitosis and produces offspring that are genetically identical to the parent.
Sexual reproduction
  • Sexual reproduction produces genetically different offspring.
  • This happens because offspring inherit genetic information from two parents.
  • The main advantage is that it creates lots of genetic variation in a population.
Asexual reproduction
  • Asexual reproduction needs only one parent.
  • It is usually fast and allows organisms to reproduce quickly.
  • The offspring are clones of the parent organism.
Advantages of asexual reproduction
  • Only one parent is needed, so the organism does not need to find a mate.
  • The process can happen very quickly.
  • A single organism can quickly colonise a new area.
  • Bacteria, fungi, and some plants can spread rapidly using asexual reproduction.
Disadvantages of asexual reproduction
  • Asexual reproduction produces little or no genetic variation.
  • If one clone is vulnerable to a disease, many or all of the clones may also be vulnerable.
  • A new disease could wipe out the whole population.
  • Cloned populations may struggle to adapt to climate change or new competitors.
Advantages of sexual reproduction
  • Sexual reproduction creates genetic variation within a population.
  • This makes a population less likely to be completely wiped out by one disease.
  • Some individuals may have traits that help them survive changing conditions.
  • Genetic variation allows evolution to take place over time.
Evolution and favourable traits
  • Evolution is the process by which species change over time.
  • Individuals with favourable traits are more likely to survive and reproduce.
  • These useful traits are more likely to be passed on to the next generation.
Disadvantages of sexual reproduction
  • Sexual reproduction takes more time and energy than asexual reproduction.
  • Animals often need to find and attract a mate.
  • Some features, such as colourful feathers in birds, may help attract mates even if they do not directly help survival.
