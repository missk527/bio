Notes (Key points from the speech)

Causes of plant disease
  • Plants can get diseases from microorganisms such as fungi, bacteria, and viruses.
  • They can also be harmed by larger organisms such as insects (e.g., aphids, spider mites).
  • Plants may also develop deficiency diseases if they lack essential mineral ions (e.g., nitrates, magnesium ions).
Nitrate deficiency
  • Nitrates are needed to make amino acids and proteins (important for growth).
  • Plants lacking nitrates often show stunted growth.
Magnesium deficiency
  • Magnesium ions are needed to make chlorophyll.
  • Without enough chlorophyll, photosynthesis is reduced and leaves may turn yellow (chlorosis).
Common symptoms of disease
  • Abnormal growths or lumps.
  • Malformed stems or leaves.
  • Patches of decay.
  • Spots/discolouration on leaves.
  • Sometimes you can directly see pests such as aphids or spider mites.
Diagnosing plant disease
  • Start with basic observations and match symptoms to a garden manual or reputable website.
  • If unsure, send a sample to a plant pathologist for detailed testing.
  • Possible tests include:
    • Microscopy of tissue samples.
    • Testing for pathogen antigens (e.g., using monoclonal antibodies).
    • DNA tests to detect pathogen DNA.
  • A simpler method is trial and error (e.g., add magnesium if you suspect deficiency; use antifungal sprays if fungal disease is suspected).
Plant defences
  • Plant defences can be grouped into physical, chemical, and mechanical defences.
Physical defences (barriers)
  • Waxy cuticle on leaves/stems helps prevent pathogen entry.
  • Cellulose cell walls around each cell.
  • Layers of dead cells such as bark around stems.
Chemical defences
  • Plants can secrete antimicrobial substances that kill bacteria/fungi.
  • They can also produce poisons that deter or kill insects.
  • Many medicines come from plant chemicals; for example, aspirin is derived from compounds found in willow bark/leaves.
Mechanical defences
  • Structures with a defensive function, such as thorns and hairs, which stop animals feeding.
  • Some leaves curl or droop when insects land on them.
Take-home message
  • Plant disease can be caused by pathogens, pests, or mineral deficiencies, and produces recognizable symptoms.
  • Diagnosis can use observation, specialist testing (microscopy/antigen/DNA), or trial-and-error treatments.
  • Plants reduce disease using physical, chemical, and mechanical defences.
