Notes

  • The kidneys filter blood and remove waste products such as urea.
  • Urea is produced in the liver from excess amino acids during deamination.
  • The kidneys also regulate levels of ions like sodium and potassium.
  • Ions are essential but must be kept at balanced levels.
  • Water balance is important to prevent cells from swelling or shrinking.
  • Water enters the body through food and drink and is lost through sweat, breathing, and urine.
  • Most water is removed from the body by the kidneys in urine.
  • Each kidney contains about a million nephrons.
  • Filtration removes small substances from the blood into kidney tubules.
  • Large molecules like proteins and cells remain in the blood.
  • Selective reabsorption returns useful substances like glucose and some water back into the blood.
  • Urea is not reabsorbed and is excreted in urine.
  • The hypothalamus monitors water concentration in the blood.
  • If water levels are low, ADH is released by the pituitary gland.
  • ADH increases water reabsorption in the kidneys, reducing urine output.
  • If water levels are high, less ADH is released, leading to more urine production.
  • This system works as a negative feedback mechanism to maintain balance.
