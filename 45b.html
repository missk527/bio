The Mole

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) Types of reproduction
  • Organisms reproduce in two main ways: sexual and asexual.
  • Some plants can use both, but most animals reproduce sexually and bacteria reproduce asexually.
2) Sexual reproduction
  • Involves fusion of male and female gametes (fertilization).
  • Requires two parents.
  • Offspring have a mix of genes from both parents.
3) Genetic variation
  • Sexual reproduction produces genetically different offspring.
  • This leads to variation in populations.
  • Variation is important for evolution and survival.
4) Gametes
  • Gametes include sperm and eggs in animals and pollen and egg cells in plants.
  • Gametes contain half the genetic material of normal cells.
  • In humans: normal cells = 46 chromosomes, gametes = 23 chromosomes.
5) Fertilization
  • When two gametes fuse, they form a cell with a full set of chromosomes.
  • This restores the chromosome number (46 in humans).
6) Asexual reproduction
  • Occurs with one parent only.
  • No gametes are involved.
  • Offspring are genetically identical clones.
7) Cell division methods
  • Mitosis is used by eukaryotes (plants, fungi, some animals).
  • Binary fission is used by prokaryotes (bacteria).
