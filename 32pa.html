Notes (Key points from the speech)

Medicines (drugs) in modern healthcare
  • Medicines (also called drugs or medications) are a major part of modern medicine.
  • Health services spend huge amounts on them (the speech mentions the NHS spending over £17 billion per year).
  • People are often treated with multiple drugs at once to improve how they feel and to treat underlying causes.
Two broad types of medication
  • Symptom-relief medicines: reduce discomfort (e.g., pain, coughing) but don’t remove the cause.
  • Disease-treating medicines: target the root cause (for example, killing bacteria).
Example: sore throat — treating symptoms
  • A sore throat can cause symptoms like coughing, throat pain, and headache.
  • Painkillers (e.g., aspirin or paracetamol) may reduce pain and ease headaches.
  • Cough medicines can reduce the urge to cough.
  • These medicines can help you feel better, but the pathogen causing the problem may still be there.
Antibiotics — when they can cure
  • Antibiotics can treat infections caused by bacteria by killing them or stopping them from multiplying.
  • They only help if the illness is bacterial.
  • Many sore throats are actually caused by viruses, so antibiotics would not help.
Why antibiotics don’t work on viruses
  • Antibiotics are designed to interfere with bacterial structures/processes, which viruses don’t have.
  • Viruses also hide inside our body’s cells, making them hard to destroy without damaging our own cells.
Choosing the right antibiotic and antibiotic resistance
  • There are many antibiotics, and each one works only against certain bacteria.
  • Doctors may need tests to identify the bacteria before prescribing the most effective antibiotic.
  • Antibiotic resistance happens when bacteria become resistant, so the same antibiotic no longer works reliably.
Big takeaway
  • Modern medicine has been transformed because we can treat both symptoms and the underlying disease.
  • Symptom-relief drugs can improve quality of life, while disease-treating drugs address root causes and can extend life.
