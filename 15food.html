Notes (English)

Sample Preparation (common to all)
  • Grind food with mortar and pestle.
  • Add distilled water in beaker, stir until partly dissolved.
  • Filter using funnel and filter paper; filtrate is test sample.
Benedict’s Test (Reducing Sugars)
  • 5 cm³ sample + 10 drops Benedict’s solution.
  • Heat in 75 °C water bath for 5 minutes.
  • Result: blue (negative), green/yellow/brick-red (positive, depends on sugar concentration).
Iodine Test (Starch)
  • 5 cm³ sample + iodine solution.
  • Result: brown-orange (negative), blue-black (positive).
Biuret Test (Protein)
  • 2 cm³ sample + 2 cm³ Biuret reagent.
  • Result: blue (negative), lilac/purple (positive).
  • Edexcel: add KOH then CuSO₄ separately.
Sudan III Test (Lipids)
  • 5 cm³ unfiltered sample + 3 drops Sudan III.
  • Result: bright red oil layer forms if lipids present.
Emulsion Test (Lipids, Edexcel)
  • Mix sample with ethanol, shake, then add equal volume of water.
  • Result: cloudy/milky white if lipids present.
