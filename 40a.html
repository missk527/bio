Notes (Key points from the speech)

Endocrine System Overview
  • The endocrine system allows different parts of the body to communicate using hormones.
  • Hormones are small chemical molecules released by glands into the bloodstream.
  • They travel around the body and only affect target cells with the correct receptors.
Pituitary Gland
  • The pituitary gland is often called the master gland.
  • It releases hormones that control other endocrine glands.
  • It is located close to and attached to the brain.
Thyroid Gland
  • The thyroid gland is located in the neck.
  • It produces the hormone thyroxine.
  • Thyroxine regulates metabolism and helps control growth and development.
Negative Feedback
  • The pituitary gland releases TSH (thyroid stimulating hormone).
  • TSH stimulates the thyroid gland to release thyroxine.
  • This keeps hormone levels stable through a process called negative feedback.
Adrenal Glands
  • Adrenal glands sit on top of the kidneys.
  • They produce adrenaline.
  • Adrenaline prepares the body for the fight‑or‑flight response by increasing heart rate and blood flow.
Pancreas
  • The pancreas produces the hormone insulin.
  • Insulin helps regulate blood glucose concentration.
Reproductive Glands
  • Testes produce testosterone and sperm cells.
  • Ovaries produce estrogen and egg cells.
  • These hormones control puberty and reproduction.
Endocrine vs Nervous System
  • The endocrine system uses hormones carried in blood.
  • The nervous system uses electrical impulses in nerves.
  • Hormone responses are slower but last longer.
  • Nerve impulses are very fast but their effects are short‑lasting.
