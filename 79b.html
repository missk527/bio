Notes (Key points rewritten from the speech)

Overview of Protein Synthesis
  • Protein synthesis occurs in two stages: transcription and translation.
  • Transcription copies DNA into mRNA.
  • Translation uses mRNA to build a protein.
DNA and Genes
  • DNA is found in the nucleus and contains genes.
  • Genes are sequences of bases that code for proteins.
  • Proteins are made from amino acids.
mRNA Structure
  • mRNA is single-stranded and shorter than DNA.
  • Contains uracil (U) instead of thymine (T).
  • Can leave the nucleus to reach the ribosome.
Transcription
  • Enzyme RNA polymerase binds to DNA.
  • DNA strands separate and bases are exposed.
  • mRNA forms by complementary base pairing (A-U, C-G).
  • The DNA strand used is called the template strand.
Translation
  • Occurs at the ribosome.
  • mRNA is read in triplets (codons).
  • Each codon codes for an amino acid.
  • tRNA brings amino acids with matching anticodons.
  • Amino acids join to form a polypeptide chain.
Final Protein Formation
  • The chain folds into a functional protein.
  • Correct sequence of amino acids is essential.
