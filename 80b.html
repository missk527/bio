Mutations

Key Notes

1) What are mutations?
  • A mutation is a change in the DNA base sequence.
  • Mutations can happen spontaneously during DNA replication.
  • Mutations occur frequently before cell division.
2) Causes of mutations
  • Carcinogens increase the risk of mutations.
  • Cigarette smoke contains harmful carcinogenic chemicals.
  • X-rays and gamma rays can also increase mutation risk.
3) Genes and proteins
  • A gene is a section of DNA that codes for a protein.
  • DNA bases are read in groups of three called codons.
  • Each codon codes for one amino acid.
4) Protein formation
  • Amino acids join together to form protein chains.
  • The chain folds into a specific shape.
  • The shape determines the protein’s function.
5) Effects of mutations
  • A mutation can change the amino acid sequence.
  • This may change the shape or function of a protein.
  • Enzymes may stop working if the active site changes shape.
6) Non-coding DNA
  • Most mutations happen in non-coding DNA.
  • Non-coding DNA does not code for proteins.
  • Some non-coding DNA controls gene expression.
7) Gene expression
  • Gene expression means a gene is switched on.
  • Different cells express different genes.
  • Nerve cells do not express the hemoglobin gene.
8) Types of mutations
  • Substitution mutations replace one base with another.
  • Insertion mutations add an extra base.
  • Deletion mutations remove a base and can shift all later codons.
