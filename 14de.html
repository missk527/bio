Digestive Enzymes & Bile

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) Overview of digestion & enzymes
  • Digestion changes large, insoluble food molecules into small, soluble molecules that can be absorbed into the blood.
  • Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up these reactions without being used up.
  • The three main nutrient groups that need enzyme digestion are carbohydrates, proteins and fats (lipids).
  • Small, soluble molecules are absorbed through the lining of the small intestine.
2) Carbohydrates & amylase
  • Carbohydrates are found in foods such as pasta, potatoes and rice.
  • The main carbohydrate that we digest is starch, which the body uses as an energy source.
  • Starch is broken down by the enzyme amylase into smaller sugars such as maltose.
  • Amylase is produced in three places:
Salivary glands (mouth) Pancreas Small intestine
3) Proteins & protease
  • Proteins are found in foods such as meat, nuts and beans.
  • Protease enzymes break proteins down into amino acids, which are used for growth and repair.
  • Proteases are made in three places:
Stomach (here the protease is called pepsin) Pancreas Small intestine
4) Fats (lipids) & lipase
  • Fats, also called lipids, are found in foods such as cheese, oils and chocolate.
  • Lipase enzymes break lipids down into glycerol and fatty acids.
  • Lipases are produced in:
Pancreas Small intestine
5) Bile: production, storage & functions
  • Bile is a digestive fluid made by the liver and stored in the gallbladder.
  • Bile is released into the small intestine.
  • Bile has two key roles:
1. Neutralises stomach acid • Stomach contents are very acidic. • Bile is alkaline, so it raises the pH. • This gives enzymes in the small intestine their optimum pH. 2. Emulsifies fats • Breaks large fat droplets into many tiny droplets. • This greatly increases the surface area of the fat. • Lipase can now work much faster.

Important: bile is not an enzyme, but it helps enzymes, especially lipase, to digest fats efficiently.

6) Memory tips
  • All three enzyme types (amylase, protease, lipase) are made in the pancreas and the small intestine.
  • Only amylase is also made in the salivary glands.
  • Only protease is also made in the stomach (where it is called pepsin).
  • Remember the pattern:
    Amylase → starch → sugars (e.g. maltose) Protease → proteins → amino acids Lipase → lipids (fats) → glycerol + fatty acids
