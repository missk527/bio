Genetic Diagrams

Key Notes

1) Alleles and Traits
  • Genes can have different forms called alleles.
  • Dominant alleles are written with capital letters.
  • Recessive alleles are written with lowercase letters.
2) Mouse Muscle Example
  • Capital A represents normal muscle.
  • Lowercase a represents muscular mice.
  • Normal muscle is dominant over muscular muscle.
3) Homozygous and Heterozygous
  • Homozygous means both alleles are the same.
  • Heterozygous means the alleles are different.
  • Recessive traits are only expressed when homozygous.
4) Genetic Diagrams
  • Genetic diagrams show possible allele combinations.
  • You identify parent genotype and phenotype first.
  • Gametes contain half the genetic information.
5) Punnett Squares
  • Punnett squares help predict offspring genotypes.
  • Gametes are placed along the top and side.
  • Each square shows one possible combination.
6) Heterozygous Crosses
  • Crossing Aa × Aa gives AA, Aa, Aa, and aa.
  • This gives three normal offspring and one muscular offspring.
  • The phenotype ratio is 3:1.
7) Ratios and Probability
  • Genetic outcomes can be written as ratios.
  • The probability of muscular offspring is 1 in 4.
  • This is equal to 25%.
8) Genes and Environment
  • Most traits are controlled by many genes.
  • The environment can affect characteristics.
  • Food and sleep can influence growth and height.
