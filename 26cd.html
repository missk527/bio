Notes (Key points rewritten from the speech)

What are microorganisms and pathogens?
  • Microorganisms: bacteria, viruses, protists, fungi.
  • Most are harmless, but a small subset are pathogens — microorganisms that cause disease.
  • Diseases caused by pathogens are communicable/infectious and affect animals and plants.
How pathogens spread (three main routes)
  • Airborne: tiny droplets from coughs/sneezes can carry viruses (e.g., influenza, measles).
  • Food & water: ingesting contaminated items (e.g., cholera via contaminated water; Salmonella via contaminated food such as chicken).
  • Direct contact: touching contaminated surfaces or skin (e.g., athlete’s foot fungi in swimming pools).
How to reduce spread (four strategies)
  • Hygiene: wash hands after the bathroom and before food prep; clean knives/chopping boards.
  • Kill vectors: eliminate organisms that carry pathogens (e.g., mosquitoes for malaria protists) — often using insecticides.
  • Vaccination: best prevention — if people don’t catch it, they can’t pass it on.
  • Isolation/quarantine: separate infected individuals (used for more serious diseases).
