2) Condensed Notes (English)

  • Role of the circulatory system: transport oxygen and nutrients to tissues; remove wastes.
  • Key components: blood (carries gases & nutrients), blood vessels (tubes), and the heart (pump).
  • Double circulation: two loops run in series.
    • Pulmonary circuit: right heart → lungs (blood gains O2) → left heart.
    • Systemic circuit: left heart → body (tissues use O2) → right heart.
  • Heart structure: four chambers — two atria (top) and two ventricles (bottom) with thick muscular walls.
  • Diagram orientation tip: In typical front-facing diagrams, the heart’s left side appears on the viewer’s right, and vice versa.
  • Valves: located between chambers and at vessel exits; they prevent backflow and keep blood moving one way.
  • Blood pathway through the heart (simplified):
    1. Deoxygenated blood from the body enters the right atrium via the vena cava.
    2. Oxygenated blood from the lungs enters the left atrium via the pulmonary veins.
    3. Atria contract → blood moves into the ventricles.
    4. Ventricles contract → blood exits via the pulmonary artery (to lungs) and the aorta (to body).
    5. Atria refill; the cycle repeats ~70 beats/min (~100,000/day).
  • Pacemaker: specialized cells in the right atrium generate electrical impulses that coordinate contraction. Faulty pacemakers can be treated with an artificial pacemaker implanted under the skin with a lead to the heart.
  • Arteries vs. veins (definition-based): arteries carry blood away from the heart; veins carry blood to the heart. Oxygen level does not define them (e.g., pulmonary artery carries deoxygenated blood; pulmonary vein carries oxygenated blood).
  • Coronary circulation: the heart muscle receives its own oxygenated blood via coronary arteries branching from the aorta, which encircle the heart.
