Notes (Key points from the speech)

Inherited disorders
  • Inherited disorders are conditions that can be passed from parents to children through certain alleles.
  • The two examples in the speech are polydactyly and cystic fibrosis.
  • Genetic diagrams can be used to predict possible offspring genotypes and phenotypes.
Polydactyly
  • Polydactyly is a condition where a baby is born with extra fingers or toes.
  • It usually does not cause other serious health problems.
  • Polydactyly is caused by a dominant allele.
  • This means a person can have polydactyly even if they inherit only one copy of the allele.
Polydactyly genetic cross
  • If a heterozygous person with polydactyly is crossed with a homozygous normal person, the offspring can be either affected or normal.
  • The possible phenotypes are in a 1:1 ratio.
  • This means there is a 50% chance of an offspring having polydactyly in this example.
Cystic fibrosis
  • Cystic fibrosis is caused by a recessive allele.
  • It is a disorder of the cell membranes.
  • It causes thick, sticky mucus to build up in parts of the body, especially the lungs and pancreas.
  • A person must inherit two recessive alleles to have cystic fibrosis.
Carriers of cystic fibrosis
  • A carrier is heterozygous: they have one normal allele and one cystic fibrosis allele.
  • Carriers do not have cystic fibrosis themselves.
  • However, carriers can pass the cystic fibrosis allele to their children.
  • In the speech, about 1 in 25 people are described as carriers.
Two carrier cross
  • If two cystic fibrosis carriers have a child, both parents have a normal phenotype but heterozygous genotype.
  • The offspring genotypes are: one homozygous dominant, two heterozygous carriers, and one homozygous recessive.
  • The phenotype ratio is 3 normal : 1 cystic fibrosis.
  • This gives a 25% chance of a child having cystic fibrosis.
Embryo screening
  • IVF allows egg cells to be fertilised by sperm in a laboratory.
  • After fertilisation, the embryo can be tested before it is implanted into the uterus.
  • Embryo screening checks the embryo’s genes for alleles linked to inherited disorders.
  • If an embryo carries alleles that may cause a disorder, parents and doctors may choose a different embryo.
Advantages and concerns of embryo screening
  • One advantage is that it can reduce suffering because fewer children are born with serious genetic disorders.
  • Another advantage is that it can reduce the cost of treating genetic disorders.
  • One concern is that it may suggest people with genetic disorders are less desirable, which could increase prejudice.
  • Another concern is that screening could be misused for non-medical traits such as eye colour, physique, or gender.
  • The speech notes that the UK has laws to help prevent this kind of misuse.
