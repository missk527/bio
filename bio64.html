  1. Land is a limited resource because there is only a finite amount available.
  2. Humans use land for building cities, quarrying, farming, and waste dumping.
  3. As more land is used by humans, less remains as habitat for other organisms.
  4. Secondary impacts are knock-on effects that spread and cause extra environmental damage.
  5. Deforestation means cutting down and removing forests.
  6. One major reason for deforestation is to clear land for farming.
  7. Deforested land may be used for raising cattle, growing animal feed, or crops such as rice.
  8. Biofuels are plant-based fuels that can be burned instead of fossil fuels.
  9. Deforestation can also happen when trees are cut down for wood, building, or fuel.
  10. Deforestation is especially common in tropical rainforest regions.
  11. Fewer trees means less photosynthesis, so less CO2 is removed from the air.
  12. The process that removes CO2 from the atmosphere is photosynthesis.
  13. Burning forests releases stored carbon back into the atmosphere as CO2.
  14. Smoke from burning can suffocate animals and pollute nearby towns and cities.
  15. Biodiversity means the variety of living organisms in an area.
  16. Deforestation destroys habitats and reduces biodiversity.
  17. If habitat loss continues, populations can shrink until species become extinct.
  18. Peat bogs are wetland habitats where peat forms in the soil.
  19. Peat bog conditions are acidic and waterlogged.
  20. These conditions slow decomposition because the soil is too acidic and lacks oxygen.
  21. Water prevents air (oxygen) from entering the soil.
  22. Peat is partially decayed plant material.
  23. Because decomposition is slow, carbon becomes stored in peat rather than released as CO2.
  24. Peat bogs store carbon, helping keep CO2 out of the atmosphere.
  25. Peat bogs are often drained to use the land for farming (cattle or crops).
  26. When drained, microorganisms return and dead plants decompose faster.
  27. This releases carbon dioxide (CO2) into the atmosphere.
  28. Burning peat or exposing it to oxygen causes stored carbon to be released as CO2.
  29. Peat is sometimes used as a fuel or in garden compost.
  30. Peat is a non-renewable resource because it forms much more slowly than it is removed.
  31. Destroying peat bogs reduces biodiversity and increases extinction risk.
  32. A carbon sink is something that removes and stores carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.
  33. Forests act as carbon sinks by absorbing CO2 in photosynthesis and storing carbon in biomass.
  34. Deforestation leads to less CO2 uptake, more CO2 release (often by burning), and habitat loss.
  35. Both forests and peat bogs can increase atmospheric CO2 if they are destroyed.
  36. Smoke causes air pollution and can harm animals and humans.
  37. Habitat destruction removes places organisms live and reproduce, leading to population declines.
  38. Waterlogged means saturated with water.
  39. Many decomposers cannot survive or work well in acidic conditions.
  40. Converting forests and peat bogs increases CO2 and reduces biodiversity, raising extinction risk.
