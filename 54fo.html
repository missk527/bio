Notes (Key points rewritten from the speech)

What are fossils and why study them?
  • Fossils are things left behind by long‑dead organisms: rock‑like skeletons, casts, impressions, or even entire preserved organisms.
  • Most organisms that ever lived are now extinct, so fossils are our main way to learn about them.
  • Fossils show how organisms changed through time — evidence for evolution via small, incremental changes over millions of years.
Three main ways fossils form
  • 1) Gradual replacement by minerals
    Slow‑decaying hard parts (bones, teeth, shells) are replaced by minerals as they break down, forming rock‑like replicas of the original structures.
  • 2) Casts & impressions
    Casts: An organism buried in soft material (e.g., clay) decays; the void left behind hardens into a mould of its shape (also seen with burrows or plant roots).
    Impressions: Preserved marks such as footprints that remain in the substrate.
  • 3) Preservation with little/no decay
    Occurs when decay is prevented: amber or tar pits (no oxygen/moisture), glaciers (too cold for decomposers), or peat bogs (too acidic).
The fossil record — very old and very incomplete
  • The oldest fossils are roughly ~3–4 billion years old.
  • The record is incomplete: there are long gaps where we don't know what happened, and we still do not know how life first began on Earth.
  • Why incomplete?
    • Many early life forms were soft‑bodied → decayed quickly, leaving few/no fossils.
    • Over immense time, fossils can be destroyed by geological processes: volcanoes, earthquakes, or movement of tectonic plates.
Extinction — when a species disappears
  • Extinction: a species has no surviving individuals.
  • Causes include:
    • Rapid environmental change (e.g., habitat destruction, rapid global warming).
    • Arrival of a new predator (including human hunting).
    • New disease (epidemic or long‑term).
    • Competition from a new species that out‑competes for resources.
    • Catastrophic events (e.g., the asteroid impact in Mexico that wiped out dinosaurs ~66 million years ago).
Core definitions for the exam
  • Cast: A hardened mould of an organism's shape left after it decays in soft sediment.
  • Impression: A preserved mark, such as a footprint.
  • Amber: Fossilised tree resin that can trap and preserve organisms by preventing decay.
  • Extinction: No individuals of a species remain.
