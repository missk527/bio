Notes (Key points from the speech)

Diffusion vs. Osmosis
  • Diffusion: particles spread from higher to lower concentration.
  • Osmosis: a special case of diffusion — the movement of water molecules across a partially permeable membrane from a region of higher water concentration to lower water concentration.
“Water concentration” & solutes
  • “Water concentration” compares the amount of water to the amount of solutes (e.g., sugars, salts) dissolved in it.
  • More solute → lower water concentration; less solute → higher water concentration.
Beaker model (thought experiment)
  • Two beakers, equal water volumes. Left has 1 g solute; right has 3 g solute.
  • Right beaker has higher solute and therefore lower water concentration.
  • With a partially permeable membrane between them, water diffuses left → right by osmosis until water concentrations equalize.
Cell membranes & typical direction
  • Cell cytoplasm contains many solutes → relatively low water concentration inside.
  • Therefore, water outside often diffuses into cells by osmosis.
Effects on animal vs. plant cells
  • Animal cells: too much water entry → cell may burst (like a balloon).
  • Plant cells: cell wall stops bursting; cell becomes turgid when full of water.
Hypertonic (very sugary) solutions
  • Outside water concentration lower than inside → water leaves the cell.
  • Excessive water loss → cell becomes dehydrated; in plants we describe it as flaccid.
