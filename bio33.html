  1. Drugs are used to relieve symptoms and help cure diseases.
  2. Drug testing studies how medicines are developed and tested before real-world use.
  3. Organisms have evolved useful chemicals over millions of years.
  4. Many useful drugs come from microorganisms and plants.
  5. Some natural substances can be used directly as drugs.
  6. Others must first be modified in a laboratory.
  7. Painkillers are used for pain relief and to reduce fevers.
  8. Aspirin was originally derived from willow bark.
  9. Digitalis is used to treat heart problems such as heart failure.
  10. Digitalis comes from the foxglove plant.
  11. These plants became valuable and were over-collected.
  12. Alexander Fleming was a microbiologist who studied bacteria.
  13. He noticed a fungus killing nearby bacterial colonies.
  14. This showed the presence of an antibacterial substance.
  15. The fungus belonged to the genus Penicillium.
  16. This discovery led to the first antibiotic.
  17. Drugs are assessed for efficacy, toxicity, and dosage.
  18. Efficacy means how well a drug works.
  19. For example, how well an antibiotic kills bacteria.
  20. Toxicity refers to how harmful a drug is.
  21. This includes cell damage and side effects.
  22. Dosage is the amount or concentration of a drug given.
  23. Increasing dosage may increase effect but also side effects.
  24. Preclinical testing begins with human cells and tissues grown in a lab.
  25. This is quick, cheap, and allows many substances to be tested.
  26. However, it does not show effects on whole organisms.
  27. The next stage is testing on live animals.
  28. Animals are used because their bodies are similar to humans.
  29. There are two main testing stages.
  30. The first stage is called preclinical testing.
  31. The second stage is clinical testing (clinical trials).
  32. Clinical trials first use healthy volunteers.
  33. This checks that the drug is safe.
  34. Researchers monitor side effects and health.
  35. The aim is to find the maximum safe dose.
  36. Later trials use patients with the target illness.
  37. The correct dose maximizes benefit and minimizes harm.
  38. A placebo is a look-alike substance with no active ingredient.
  39. In double-blind trials, neither doctors nor patients know who receives the drug.
  40. This ensures fair testing and prevents false claims.
