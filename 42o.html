Notes

  • Insulin is a hormone released by the pancreas when blood glucose levels are too high.
  • It helps cells absorb glucose and convert it into glycogen, lowering blood sugar levels.
  • Diabetes occurs when the body cannot properly regulate blood glucose due to problems with insulin.
  • Type 1 diabetes usually develops in childhood and is lifelong.
  • In Type 1 diabetes, the pancreas produces little or no insulin.
  • Patients must inject insulin to control blood glucose levels.
  • The amount of insulin needed depends on diet and exercise.
  • High sugar intake requires more insulin.
  • Exercise reduces blood glucose as muscles absorb more glucose.
  • Type 2 diabetes usually develops in adults with unhealthy lifestyles.
  • In Type 2 diabetes, cells become resistant to insulin.
  • The pancreas may still produce insulin, but it is ineffective.
  • Main treatment for Type 2 is a healthy diet and regular exercise.
